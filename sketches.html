The concept of a random walk is a stochastic process by which (pseudo)randomly-moving objects wander away from where they started, each direction they step being a random choice among a series of options with equal probability. Each time the sketch is played, it generates a different series of steps. It is never the same.

part of a series of graduate research on Randomness for Dan Shiffman's Nature of Code curriculum, Reimagining the P5 curriculum, equitable working group - equitable syllabus project
