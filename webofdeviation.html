Web of Deviations

research on the notion of the desire line as a material, conceptual and emergent phenomenon

A desire line is an informal path created by erosion caused by repeated use. They “can be found all over the city and all over the world - anywhere people want to walk, where no formal paths have been provided.

Desire lines often emerge as shortcuts - deviations onto grass away from pavement. However, they can arise due to a multiplicity of reasons. They are traces of use or wear that indicate preferred methods of interaction with places or systems. As emergent urbanisms/architectures, in the words of one journal, they 'record collective disobedience'”(2).

In this sense, the force of desire is productive in its ability to reorient, fuel and generate alternative landscapes. But desire is not a one-way street. We both make places and are made by them through a continuous co-constitutive, relational process.

How does desire create surfaces and boundaries? How are we shaped by its e/affects? How does governed space prescript our movement? How do the structures in our lives make some things reachable and others out of reach? How might we craft insurgent desire paths toward more just and pleasurable worlds?

Process

Using flickr scrape, I trained a model in runway ML and produced several experiments. Using these images below from open flickr group 'Desire paths' as input data, I trained a type of image-generating machine learning model called a GAN, which generates new data that resembles a given dataset - learning the patterns in the data, and 'teaching' the machine to cause the pattern to emerge.
